# Editing Expert

## Role

The editing expert shapes time, rhythm, clarity, and retention. The main question is: where should the viewer's attention move next?

## Core Thinking

- Cut on intention, reaction, motion, or new information.
- Remove repeated information unless repetition escalates.
- Protect cause and effect.
- Use pacing changes to signal emotional shifts.
- Let reaction shots carry meaning.
- The edit should make imperfect AI clips feel purposeful.
- Prioritize the cut in this order: emotion, story, rhythm, eye trace, screen plane, then spatial continuity.
- Use retention data as a diagnosis tool after publishing or test screening.

## AI Short Drama Rules

- Build a rough cut early using placeholder shots.
- Keep alternate generated clips for emotional beats.
- Use inserts to hide continuity problems.
- Use J-cuts and L-cuts when dialogue or sound can smooth visual discontinuity.
- Review rhythm at actual target platform size and speed.
- If retention drops early, revise the opening promise, conflict reveal, or first visual question.
- If retention spikes, inspect whether the moment is genuinely compelling or merely confusing.

## Outputs

- Assembly edit plan.
- Cut notes.
- Retention pass.
- Transition plan.
- Subtitle timing notes.

## Review Questions

- Does every cut give new information or stronger emotion?
- Are there dead seconds?
- Is the story still clear without sound?
- Do subtitles land before the viewer needs them?
- Can a bad AI transition be solved with an insert, reaction, or sound bridge?
- Does the cut preserve emotion and story even if spatial continuity is imperfect?

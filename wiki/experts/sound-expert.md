# Sound Expert

## Role

The sound expert designs clarity, emotional contour, and continuity through dialogue, ambience, music, and effects.

## Core Thinking

- Dialogue clarity comes first.
- Sound can connect shots that do not visually match perfectly.
- Music should support emotional turns, not cover unclear storytelling.
- Ambient sound makes AI visuals feel grounded.
- Silence can create pressure when used deliberately.
- Sound should influence story, staging, editing, and pacing early, not only polish the final cut.
- Sound can suggest off-screen space, character psychology, memory, threat, or transition.

## AI Short Drama Rules

- Plan sound cues during storyboard, not only after editing.
- Use consistent ambience for the same location.
- Keep voice identity stable across episodes.
- Use audio bridges to smooth AI visual jumps.
- Check subtitle timing against spoken rhythm.
- Add sound intent to scene cards and shot lists before clip generation.
- Use recurring sonic motifs for important characters, locations, or emotional states.

## Outputs

- Dialogue cleanup notes.
- Music cue sheet.
- Ambience map.
- Sound effect list.
- Audio continuity review.

## Review Questions

- Can dialogue be understood on a phone speaker?
- Does the music change at the right emotional beat?
- Are location ambience and room tone consistent?
- Is sound hiding or revealing a story problem?
- Did sound design help shape the scene, or was it only added after the edit was already fixed?

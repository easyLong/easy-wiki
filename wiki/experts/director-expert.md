# Director Expert

## Role

The director expert owns interpretation, staging, tone, and performance. The main question is: what is the intended audience experience of this scene?

## Core Thinking

- Define the emotional center of the scene before designing shots.
- Give characters playable objectives.
- Stage action so power relationships are visible.
- Keep tone consistent with genre.
- Treat AI output as performance material that must be selected and shaped, not blindly accepted.
- Hold the whole story so each scene, even if generated separately, points toward the same emotional and narrative direction.
- Ground staging decisions in character motivation, not arbitrary camera coverage.

## AI Short Drama Rules

- Generate multiple performance variants for key emotional beats.
- Reject clips where body language contradicts the script.
- Use scene cards to align script, storyboard, and edit.
- Make character intention visible in prompt language.
- Before generation, define what each actor wants, what they hide, and what changes in the scene.
- Avoid excessive coverage when a smaller number of stronger shots better expresses the scene.

## Outputs

- Scene intention.
- Character direction.
- Blocking notes.
- Performance selection notes.
- Tone guardrails.

## Review Questions

- What should the audience feel at the end of the scene?
- Does the actor behavior match the character's objective?
- Is the power dynamic visible?
- Does the generated clip fit the genre tone?
- Is staging motivated by character pressure, not just visual variety?

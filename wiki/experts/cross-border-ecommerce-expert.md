# Cross-Border Ecommerce Expert

## Role

The cross-border ecommerce expert designs market entry, channel strategy, marketplace control, fulfillment, localization, and conversion tests for brands selling outside their home market.

The main question is: what must be true before this brand should commit more inventory, ad spend, compliance cost, or operational complexity?

## Core Thinking

- Validate demand before scaling. Use virtual tests when inventory does not exist, and physical small-batch tests when product-market fit or reviews must be proven.
- Match channels to intent: Facebook can create awareness, Google can capture research and consideration, Amazon can capture comparison and purchase.
- Control the brand on marketplaces. If a product is popular, unauthorized sellers may shape listings, pricing, reviews, and customer expectations.
- Make landed cost transparent. Customers punish surprise duties, taxes, pickups, and delivery friction more than they reward vague promises.
- Treat listing optimization as continuous testing, not a one-time launch task.
- Keep fundamentals ahead of tactics: product selection, positioning, keyword fit, conversion, customer service, and unit economics come before platform hacks.
- Localize trust, not just language. Brand clarity, cultural expectations, compliance, pricing display, and customer support all affect conversion.

## Outputs

- Market-entry readiness review.
- Channel and funnel map.
- Marketplace control plan.
- Fulfillment and landed-cost plan.
- Listing and conversion test plan.
- Localization and compliance risk notes.

## Review Questions

- What evidence proves demand in this target market?
- Is the channel being used for the buyer intent it is strongest at?
- Who controls marketplace listings, pricing, reviews, and brand presentation?
- Does the customer see the true total cost before purchase?
- Which conversion metric is below category or channel benchmark?
- What must be learned from the next small test before scaling?
- Which tactic is platform-specific and likely to expire?

# Walter Murch - Rule of Six

## Summary

Walter Murch's Rule of Six gives an editor a hierarchy for choosing a cut. The usual summary ranks the criteria as emotion, story, rhythm, eye trace, two-dimensional screen plane, and three-dimensional spatial continuity.

## Useful Ideas

- Emotion and story should outrank technical continuity when they conflict.
- Rhythm is not just speed; it is whether the cut lands at the right moment.
- Eye trace matters because cuts should guide the viewer's attention.
- Screen direction and spatial continuity matter, but they are lower-level constraints than emotion and story.

## Implications For AI Short Drama

AI-generated clips often have continuity problems. This framework helps decide whether to regenerate, cut around, or accept a flaw:

- If emotion and story fail, regenerate or rewrite.
- If rhythm fails, trim, extend, or reorder.
- If eye trace fails, use an insert, reaction, or reframing.
- If only spatial continuity fails, consider hiding it with a reaction shot, insert, or sound bridge.

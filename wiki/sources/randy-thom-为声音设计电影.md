# Randy Thom - Designing A Movie For Sound

## Summary

Randy Thom argues that sound design is not merely creating impressive effects. Sound should participate in story design early and influence decisions across writing, directing, editing, music, and mixing.

## Useful Ideas

- Great sound is not the same as loud sound.
- Sound can shape the picture as much as picture shapes the sound.
- Sound can suggest mood, clarify plot, define character, connect moments, smooth transitions, and create ambiguity.
- Films benefit when sound is considered from the script and production design stage, not only in post.

## Implications For AI Short Drama

- Add sound intent to [[scene-card-template|scene cards]] and [[shot-list-template|shot lists]].
- Use ambience and sound bridges to hide AI visual discontinuities.
- Design audio motifs for characters, locations, and emotional states.
- Do not wait until the final export to think about voice, music, and ambience.

# DGA Interviews - Directing Craft

## Summary

DGA interviews with working directors emphasize preparation, actor motivation, tone, staging, and holding the whole story during production.

## Useful Ideas

- Dan Attias describes directing as finding what he deeply cares about in the story and making scenes about something bigger than surface action.
- He emphasizes holding the whole story for actors who are shooting out of sequence.
- Staging choices should answer actor questions: why start here, move there, or consolidate coverage?
- Visual references and repeated walkthroughs can keep departments aligned.

## Implications For AI Short Drama

- The [[director-expert]] should define scene intention before prompting.
- Scene cards should include character motivation, not only visible action.
- AI-generated performances should be reviewed against the character objective.
- Coverage should be practical: do not generate nine shots when three stronger shots can carry the scene.

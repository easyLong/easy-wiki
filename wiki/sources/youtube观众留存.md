# YouTube Help - Audience Retention

## Summary

YouTube's audience retention report identifies how different moments in a video hold viewer attention. It highlights intros, top moments, spikes, and dips.

## Useful Ideas

- Intro performance shows how many viewers are still watching after the first 30 seconds.
- Flat sections indicate viewers are watching through that part.
- Gradual declines suggest viewers are losing interest over time.
- Spikes can mean a segment is rewatched, shared, or unclear.
- Dips show moments where viewers skip or stop watching.

## Implications For AI Short Drama

Use retention as a post-release editing teacher:

- If early drop-off is high, test a faster conflict reveal or clearer promise.
- If a later top moment works, consider moving its dramatic value earlier.
- If a spike appears, decide whether the moment is compelling or confusing.
- If a dip appears, inspect repetition, unclear story logic, slow pacing, bad audio, or visual mismatch.

# Cross-Border Ecommerce Domain

## Purpose

This domain captures reusable knowledge for brands selling across markets, platforms, fulfillment networks, and regulatory boundaries.

It is not an app project. It is a durable knowledge area that can support future projects through the wiki service.

## Core Model

Cross-border ecommerce is a coupled system:

- Demand validation: prove the market wants the product before committing inventory.
- Channel strategy: match Facebook, Google, Amazon, DTC, retail, and marketplace channels to the customer journey.
- Marketplace control: decide how the brand appears, who is allowed to sell, and how listings stay accurate.
- Fulfillment and customs: design the customer experience around delivery promises, duties, taxes, returns, and local compliance.
- Listing and conversion: keep testing price, images, Q&A, value proposition, and conversion benchmarks.
- Brand localization: make the offer clear across language, culture, trust expectations, and platform norms.

## Operating Principles

- Do not assume a domestic playbook travels unchanged; foreign-market failure often comes from weak preparation and misread demand.
- Treat virtual tests, small-batch physical tests, and marketplace launches as learning loops before scaling.
- A channel is not only a traffic source; it represents a different buyer intent stage.
- Marketplace sales require brand-control strategy because unauthorized sellers and weak listings can shape customer perception.
- Fast international shipping is not always the goal; transparent landed cost and low-friction delivery can matter more than speed.
- Platform tactics are useful only after fundamentals are in place: product selection, brand position, keyword/search fit, customer service, and unit economics.

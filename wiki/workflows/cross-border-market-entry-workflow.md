# Cross-Border Market Entry Workflow

## Purpose

Test and enter a foreign ecommerce market without confusing early signal, platform tactics, and scalable operating model.

## Trigger

Use this workflow when a brand wants to sell in a new country, marketplace, or channel and has not yet proven demand, fulfillment feasibility, and marketplace control.

## Steps

1. Define the target customer, product promise, price band, channel, and success metric.
2. Run requirement intake for constraints: inventory, compliance, budget, launch date, fulfillment, returns, support, and non-goals.
3. Choose a test mode:
   - Virtual test when inventory is not committed and the question is interest or message resonance.
   - Physical small-batch test when the question is product acceptance, reviews, repeat purchase, or retail readiness.
   - Marketplace test when the question is search demand, conversion, or competitive position.
4. Map the channel role:
   - Facebook or social: awareness and prospecting.
   - Google: search and consideration.
   - Amazon or marketplace: comparison and purchase.
   - DTC: brand control, customer data, bundling, and repeat purchase.
5. Design the fulfillment and landed-cost experience before scale.
6. Check marketplace control: authorized sellers, listing ownership, trademark, brand registry, review policy, and pricing leakage.
7. Run the smallest test that can answer the scaling question.
8. Review results by signal quality, not vanity metrics.
9. Update positioning, listing, pricing, creative, fulfillment, or compliance plan.
10. Scale only after the bottleneck is named and the next risk is acceptable.

## Promotion Criteria

Move from test to scale only when:

- Demand signal is tied to purchase intent or reliable conversion proxy.
- Fulfillment cost, duties, taxes, delivery time, and returns are understood.
- Marketplace control is acceptable.
- Unit economics survive realistic ad and fulfillment costs.
- The brand message is clear in the target culture and channel.

## Failure Modes

- Treating traffic or email signups as purchase proof.
- Copying a domestic launch playbook without local compliance and behavior checks.
- Scaling ad spend before listing conversion is near benchmark.
- Letting unauthorized sellers define the brand.
- Hiding duties or taxes until delivery.
- Confusing one platform hack with a repeatable operating capability.
